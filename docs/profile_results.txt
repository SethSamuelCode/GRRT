Analysis
--------
The DOMINANT bottleneck is the outer geodesic integration loop, which
accounts for 95.5% of all CPU time.

Each trace averages 237 adaptive RK4 steps. Each adaptive step does
step-doubling error control: one full step + two half steps = 3x step_kerr
= 12 calls to derivatives_kerr (Christoffel symbol computation) per
adaptive step.

Total metric evaluations per frame:
  518400 traces × 237 steps × 12 derivatives = ~1.47 billion

The volumetric raymarcher is only 4.5% of total time. Within it:
  - Physics (density, velocity, opacity LUTs, radiative transfer): 79.4%
  - RK2 geodesic steps: 20.6%
  - Only 0.59 raymarch entries per trace (most rays miss the disk)
  - Average 25 steps per raymarch entry, 11 of which do physics

Optimization Targets (in priority order)
-----------------------------------------
1. OUTER LOOP — adaptive_step_kerr (95.5% of time)
   - Reduce step count: larger steps far from the black hole (r >> 10M)
     where spacetime is nearly flat. Current adaptive tolerance may be
     overly conservative for far-field.
   - Cheaper adaptive scheme: RK2 step-doubling (4 derivatives instead
     of 12 per adaptive step) or embedded RK methods (e.g. RK45
     Dormand-Prince, error estimate for free — 6 derivatives total).
   - Analytic geodesics for Schwarzschild (exact elliptic integrals) —
     not applicable to Kerr.
   - Carter constant integration: for Kerr, use conserved quantities
     (E, L, Q) to reduce the ODE system or predict when midplane
     crossings occur without stepping.
   - Fast far-field: switch to straight-line propagation beyond some
     r_flat where curvature is negligible, only enter full GR integration
     when r < r_flat.

2. RAYMARCH PHYSICS (3.6% of total time)
   - density_cgs() internally re-calls scale_height(r) and z_max_at(r)
     which are already computed in the raymarcher. A version accepting
     pre-computed H/zm would eliminate redundant interp_radial calls.
   - Noise (FBM, 2 octaves) in density — could reduce to 1 octave or
     remove in preview mode.

3. LOAD IMBALANCE
   - Disk-hitting pixels (near equator) are ~100x more expensive than
     sky pixels. OpenMP schedule(guided) helps but is not optimal.
     A work-stealing queue or tiled approach with priority for disk
     pixels would improve thread utilization.
